
============================================================
SQUAT
============================================================

Most frequently modified groups (LG):
RF_L_gyr, Ham_L_gyr, TA_L_acc, Gast_L_acc, Gast_R_acc, TA_R_acc

Biomechanical Interpretation:
Squat corrections emphasize bilateral quadriceps (RF) and hamstrings (Ham)
for symmetric knee flexion control and rotational stability. Modifications
to both accelerometer and gyroscope signals indicate corrections to both
linear and angular motion patterns, ensuring balanced weight distribution
during descent and ascent phases.

============================================================
KNEE EXTENSION
============================================================

Most frequently modified groups (LG):
Gast_R_acc, TA_L_acc, RF_L_acc, Gast_L_acc, Ham_R_acc, RF_R_acc

Biomechanical Interpretation:
Knee extension corrections highlight injured-side quadriceps (RF) engagement
and reduced hamstring co-contraction to address range-of-motion deficits.
Tibialis anterior (TA) and gastrocnemius (Gast) modifications suggest
adjustments to ankle stabilization during the extension phase, preventing
compensatory movements that limit full knee extension.

============================================================
GAIT
============================================================

Most frequently modified groups (LG):
RF_R_gyr, TA_R_gyr, Gast_L_gyr, TA_R_acc, Gast_L_acc, Gast_R_gyr

Biomechanical Interpretation:
Gait corrections involve coordinated quadriceps-hamstrings control during
stance phase and tibialis anterior-gastrocnemius activation for swing
clearance and push-off. High activation across all bilateral muscle groups
reflects the complex inter-limb coordination required for normal gait
kinematics, particularly in correcting asymmetric loading patterns.
